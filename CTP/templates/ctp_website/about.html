About Us

The Cottier Chamber Project is part of Glasgow's West End Festival. Running since 2011, the series aims to:

  • bring top quality professional chamber music to Glasgow's West End
  • showcase the variety and depth of talent in Scotland
  • promote and introduce chamber music to new and existing audiences
  • entertain, challenge, inspire and satisfy audiences and performers
  • have a great time listening to and playing great music!

In partnership with Four Acres Charitable Trust, Cottier’s hosts the main evening series, which is made up of concerts that last for around an hour. As the programme is packed with a huge variety of ensembles and composers, this means that you can put together your own programme to suit your tastes, try something completely new (and if you don’t like it, it’s only an hour of your life that you won’t get back!), take in two completely contrasting performances in one evening with a meal in between, or simply take pot luck and see what happens.

With descriptions ranging from 'the music of friends' to 'four men in a room, conversing rationally', it's easy to see why chamber music is so powerful! It’s where ideas are thrown around, examined, rejected and approved...where emotions of every type are worn on the sleeve...where musicians can speak for themselves, communicating with their colleagues and with the audience.

Artistic Director

Born in Dundee, Andy grew up in Staffordshire where he began horn lessons as an excuse to miss science lessons! He studied Music at York University before moving back north to Glasgow to complete his Masters degree at the RSAMD with Hugh Potts. Soon after graduating, he was appointed as principal horn of the Slovenian National Opera and Ballet Orchestra in Ljubljana, Slovenia. Since returning to Glasgow, he plays regularly with the BBC Scottish Symphony Orchestra, Scottish Chamber Orchestra, Royal Scottish National Orchestra, Scottish Opera and the Rednote Ensemble. As a soloist he has performed most of the standard repertoire concertos for the instrument in venues ranging from muddy fields to concert halls and churches. As well as running The Cottier Chamber Project, Andy is also the horn player for host ensemble Daniel’s Beard.

Planning & Development

Manager by day, sax player by night, Judith likes to collaborate with creative artists to produce concerts, tours and festivals that are good for the soul. She is currently the General Manager for eclectic ensemble Mr McFall's Chamber and for creative commissioning organisation Remembered/Imagined, and combines this with specialist development work for a wide roster of clients including Delphian Records, Tinderbox Orchestra, Ludus Baroque as well as individual artists. Her key interests lie in growing small artist-led groups, focusing on strategic planning and organisational development and providing practical support for Artistic Directors. After graduating from the Royal Northern College of Music, Judith worked in production roles for Manchester Camerata, Scottish Ensemble and Live Music Now Scotland and has sat on a number of Boards supporting the development of chamber music in Scotland.

PR

With eighteen years experience in the classical music industry, press consultant Jane Nicolson began her career in London working for the artists management agency, Askonas Holt then moved into PR working for the illustrious Dvora Lewis. With Dvora Lewis, she handled the PR for organisations such as the London Symphony Orchestra and Verbier Festival and artists such as Anne Sophie Mutter and Rostropovich. Returning to Scotland in 2006 as press manager for Scottish Opera, she became a freelance consultant in 2008 and has since worked on PR for music festivals, projects and artists such as The Cumnock Tryst, Hebrides Ensemble, Linn Records, Lammermuir Festival, Manchester Camerata, Oxford May Music and The Queen's Medal for Music. She regularly gives training days, PR 'MOTs' and consultancies to various organisations throughout the UK and produces podcasts. Jane has been on the Boards of Mendelssohn on Mull and NOISE (New Opera In Scotland Events) and is an alumnus of the University of St Andrews where she studied modern languages.

Intern

Graduating in 2006 from The Royal Scottish Conservatoire with a Masters degree in saxophone performance Karen is the soprano saxophonist in the flourishing saxophone quartet Sax Ecosse. Highlights of their career included being invited to perform in this year's Edinburgh International Festival, performing in The Usher Hall Emerging Artsits Series and entertaining many dignitaries’ including Bill Clinton, Prince Edward and Cherie Blair. In 2011 Karen was recipient of the Creative Scotland Professional Development Award enabling her to study with soprano saxophonist Christine Rall, of the Rascher Saxophone Quartet. She now enjoys a varied freelance career performing around the UK and has performed with the Royal Scottish National Orchestra and the BBC Scottish Symphony Orchestra. Away from music Karen enjoys skiing, running and learning how to take better pictures with her camera.
